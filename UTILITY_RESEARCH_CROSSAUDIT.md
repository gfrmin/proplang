## 2. Gaps the research exposes (each with a proposed disposition; every
disposition is the author's call)

### G1 — the unprinted exogenous-drift assumption (the manipulation channel)

UWalk makes preference change a sentence (§6: coherence-per-time). But
nothing in HOSTS_D_PACK, HOSTS_PLAN, or the Task-2 pack prints that the
drift is ACT-INDEPENDENT — verified by search, 2026-07-09: no occurrence
of exogenous / act-independent / natural-evolution / manipulation in any
of the three. The research (Carroll et al. 2022; Farquhar–Carey–Everitt
path-specific objectives) shows what the vacancy costs in general: an
EU-maximiser over preferences it can influence acquires the incentive to
shift them to be easier to satisfy.

Today the assumption is TRUE BY CONSTRUCTION: the wire has no act→
preference edge, the deference channel is passive, and UWalk's rate is
world-enumerated content the agent's acts cannot reach. But "true by
construction and unprinted" is exactly the vacancy-behind-a-name failure
mode the repaired residue discipline (§7, 2026-07-09) exists to catch —
the construction could change at a future boundary (any affordance whose
outcome feeds the owner's preference state, e.g. ask-fatigue) and nothing
would flag that the guard fell due.

**Proposal (author's call): a register line R-D18** — "UWalk drift is
declared act-independent: the natural-evolution baseline is apparatus,
printed under bucket-three item 1's neighbourhood beside the independence
declaration (R-D13). If any future boundary gives the agent a causal
channel into the owner's preference state, a CAUSAL (path-specific) guard
falls due — not a syntactic non-mention rule, which the research shows is
neither necessary nor sufficient." Grade II (irreducible in kind today,
gate named). One sentence in §7 item 1's neighbourhood; no oracle change
required, since the current world class cannot express the edge.

### G2 — a second, distinct deference floor: the bit-price floor

The pack's deference floor is DRIFT-FUNDED (gHeadline, D.hs:554-567:
VoI(k=12) ≥ 1e-6 under UWalk, ≤ 1e-12 in the static twin). The research
derives a different floor: utility hypotheses that agree on every emitted
observation but diverge off-path have likelihood ratio exactly 1 on all
evidence, so their posterior odds sit at their prior odds forever —
2^(−Δ bits) under the dl-prior. On-path evidence can never separate them;
only the alphabet's pricing does.

These are complementary mechanisms, not the same fact: the drift floor is
about a posterior that never concentrates IN TIME; the bit-price floor is
about a posterior that never concentrates ACROSS off-path rivals. In D's
finite-grid architecture the second is a derivable identity of the same
family as the O1/gLedger pins (an empty-cell fact at the hypothesis-pair
grain rather than the component-channel grain).

**Proposal (author's call): one candidate oracle pin, pre-freeze.** A
paired world in which two utility hypotheses agree on the likelihood of
every tick the episode emits and differ only on an unvisited context; pin
posterior-odds == prior-odds at 1e-12 after the episode, the prior odds
read off the grids' dl-prices. Legal now — the oracle is builder-owned
until the tag — and cheap (gLedger's machinery, one new world). If
declined, no gap remains open: the identity follows from the same frozen
verbs the O1 pins exercise; the pin would make it a regression rather
than a theorem-on-paper. Either ruling is coherent; the register should
record which.

### G3 — positivity is load-bearing but recorded nowhere

The USay payload's signs are fixed in the sentence (u(a,y) = −spend(y) −
θ_ask·1[a=ask] − θ_bad·badness(y), pack §3) and every residual grid is
positive (askGrid = {0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.4}, D.hs:669; fixture value/rate
grids likewise). Jointly these make the A&M sign-flip twin (−p_g, −R_π)
INEXPRESSIBLE — the research's "charity as an alphabet fact" (its
positive-rational-scaling rule), realized here as grid geometry plus
payload shape. The research's deletion proof transfers exactly: admit
negative scaling and the harmful degeneracy re-enters as an utterable
sentence no prior can then exclude (A&M's own result).

Currently this reads as an incidental drafting choice of R-D8's fixtures.

**Proposal (author's call): R-D8's ruling language records positivity as
deletion-proof-bearing** — the geometry is not just "the invariance
constant over value-space" but the charity restriction itself, in the
sense design.md's per-terminal deletion proofs (design.md:54) give that
word. No code change; the grids already comply.

### G4 — KSV state-dependent utility, located (caveat, not a change)

Karni–Schmeidler–Vind 1983: with state-dependent utility, probability and
utility are not jointly identifiable from preferences over acts; the
separation needs non-behavioural data. Located against the ledger: the
measured-$ outcome channel is precisely what escapes KSV where it reaches
(measured units anchor the representation; §5's "the accounting layer
already part-fixes scale"), and where it cannot reach (the non-$
components θ_ask, θ_bad-non-$), the comparison channel's declared-
probability lotteries are the KSV-prescribed non-behavioural instrument.
The ledger's shape is therefore the KSV answer, arrived at independently.
Worth one caveat line in the increment report so the floor is named:
components with neither measured units nor a comparison route would sit
under KSV's non-identifiability, and the ledger's empty-row pins are what
keep such a component from silently pretending otherwise.

### G5 — constructed preferences / the principal who does not know θ

Research open problem 1 (Lichtenstein–Slovic; Shah et al.'s own
limitation): if the owner is INFERRING their own θ at elicitation time,
the verdict/comparison likelihood should run through the owner's belief
b_H(θ), which is non-stationary and endogenous — UWalk models drift, not
self-inference, and the τ-owner models error, not construction.

**Proposal (author's call): fold into R-D10's standing line** — the
pointer's boundary becomes "revealed vs idealized vs CONSTRUCTED
preference"; recorded open, no update rule fixes it, exactly R-D10's
existing posture with one word added. The elicitation design partially
mitigates in practice (lotteries over measured outcomes are the question
form most robust to construction effects), but mitigation is not
identification and should not be recorded as such.

## 3. Where the pack is right and the research is loose (a guard against
over-absorption)

- **The proposed grammar is not D.** `cond-nat`, `would-have`, `⊓`/`⊔`,
  `boltzmann`-as-sentence are a richer fragment than D's USay+residual
  design. Every one of those terminals is new alphabet scope: the P5
  single-site clause stands armed, and adoption means a new roadmap
  boundary with its own oracle-first freeze — the research's own
  recommendation 5 ("ship with fixed Θ, stage the open problems") agrees
  with this staging. Nothing in the research argues any of them is needed
  for D's measured demand.
- **Its bit-pricing is hand-waved.** Flat "1 bit" per terminal and prose
  deletion proofs are the shape of the discipline, not the discipline:
  design.md prices terminals through executed code-level ablations
  (design.md:326) and grids as priced data. If a future boundary adopts
  any of the grammar, the prices are re-derived under proplang's rules,
  not imported.
- **Its manipulation critique is right in general, wrong in emphasis for
  D.** The research is correct that a syntactic may-not-mention-own-acts
  rule is neither necessary nor sufficient and the true constraint is
  causal. But D needs no guard MACHINERY — the world class cannot express
  the act→preference edge. What D needs is the printed assumption (G1).
  Building `would-have` and a d-separation side-condition now would be
  scope no measured demand has earned; the correct artifact today is one
  register sentence with a named gate.

## 4. Implications for the five open rulings (annotations, not answers)

- **R-D6 (elicitation cost values + question inventory):** the research
  confirms the lottery form (vNM/Ramsey: cardinality is bought with
  gambles, a theorem not a preference) and its worked example — "one
  certain small interruption vs p chance of $X wasted" — is a ready-made
  first inventory entry, already denominated in R-D5's measured
  observables. The rider-1 numeraire discipline is untouched by anything
  in the research.
- **R-D7 (gauge anchors):** declaration-not-estimation confirmed at
  Grade I strength (every likelihood is affine-invariant — the research's
  Savage/AA ceiling). The dangling reference-bad referent should be a
  MEASURED OUTCOME-VECTOR EVENT (a fixed $ waste; a declared red-team
  case executing), never a preference statement — a preference-flavoured
  anchor would smuggle an estimate into a coordinate.
- **R-D8 (residual grid geometries):** record positivity as load-bearing
  (G3). The fixture geometries themselves are unaffected.
- **R-D12 (wire v2 error semantics):** unaffected.
- **R-D13 (independence declaration):** confirmed. The research's "the
  complexity prior does no work on-path" is the same reason rider 2 gave
  for preferring declared product form over joint enumeration: a joint
  prior would spend correlation the data cannot discipline. The
  joint-enumeration alternative stays named-and-declined.
